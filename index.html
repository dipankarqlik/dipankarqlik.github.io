Dipankar Mazumdar

I am currently a Research & Development Advocate at Qlik, where I primarily experiment with Qlik's Associative Engine to develop and advocate on state-of-the-art possibilities (in the field of Visual Analytics, Machine Learning). I have worked in Software engineering roles during the beginning of my career and later on moved to more data-specific roles such as Visualization Engineer, Data Science Engineer. In general, I am interested in impactful data products that help people solve critical problems.

I am also a Visual Analytics researcher at the Visual Analytics and Visualization(VAV) lab part of the Faculty of Computer Science at Dalhousie University, Canada. My research is focused towards developing visualization techniques to understand Machine Learning models as well as multivariate data sets. Some of the tools & programming languages that I use in my work and research are: Python, NodeJS, D3.js, Qlik Sense, Plotly, Amazon SageMaker. I am also a community person and love sharing my work in my socials, Qlik Community blog and my hobby blog.

I completed my MSc in Computer Science at Dalhousie University, where I wrote my thesis on "Interpretability of Random Forest models"
